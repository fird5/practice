üîë What is Lateral Thinking? (Your Competitive Edge)

Lateral thinking is your secret weapon in this competition. It's a method of solving problems by **breaking away from traditional, step-by-step logic**. Instead of digging a single hole deeper, you find a completely new place to dig.

We provide a **Lateral Thinking Input** to force this creative shift. This will be an absurd or unrelated starting point that you must connect to the challenge. This process of **"forced connection"** is what will help you generate truly original and unexpected ideas.

Example: If the challenge is to "improve urban transportation," the input might be: **"PO, all cars should have square wheels."** You must then use the principles of "square wheels" (vertical movement, separating flows) to create a feasible solution.

The Duel Challenges: Choose Wisely

Your assigned challenge for the duel will be **Challenge 15: The "Smartphone Focus Hub" OR Challenge 18: The "Community Connection Broker"**.

Challenge 15: The "Smartphone Focus Hub"

Problem Narrative: Uncontrolled smartphone use and app addiction contribute to poor mental health and lack of focus. The challenge is to use the device itself to **encourage concentration, not distraction**.

Lateral Thinking Input: **Design Rule: The 'Empty Stage' Rule**
The phone's screen must be designed like a **theater stage** where every background element is stripped away to focus solely on the primary task.

Conceptual Cohesion: 40 pts (How effectively is the **'Empty Stage' Rule** translated into a functional design that encourages focus?)

Articulative Ingenuity: 35 pts (How well does the explanation justify the **effectiveness** of this digital environment in preventing distraction and encouraging genuine focus?)

Visual Impact: 25 pts (Is the digital minimalist aesthetic compelling and visually distinct from standard 'Do Not Disturb' modes?)

Challenge 18: The "Community Connection Broker"

Problem Narrative: Social anxiety prevents youth from engaging in local community activities, leading to isolation. Design a system that **removes the anxiety of the "first step"**.

Lateral Thinking Input: **Design Rule: The 'Jigsaw Puzzle' Rule**
The community and the user must be framed as a large **jigsaw puzzle**, where the app visually matches the user's "shape/skills" to the exact empty "piece/need" in their local area.

Conceptual Cohesion: 40 pts (How effectively is the **'Jigsaw Puzzle' Rule** used to break down the psychological barrier of social anxiety and shame?)

Articulative Ingenuity: 35 pts (How well does the explanation justify the **effectiveness** of the system in providing enough structure and safety to overcome the user's fear of judgment?)

Visual Impact: 25 pts (Is the visual design of the puzzle effective in conveying the sense of purpose and belonging?)
