What You'll Learn

The art of the Lovable prompt is not about technical specification — it is about describing the user experience you want to create. Arjun's 30-Minute Build Framework gives you a structured sequence that moves from concept to live URL without getting stuck on decisions that don't matter yet.

You will build your first app live in Lovable.dev — a minimal viable marketplace — and learn to use the iterative chat interface to add features one at a time without breaking what already works.

The 30-Minute Build Framework
1
0–5 MIN
Write the one-sentence brief
Define the product in one sentence: who it's for, what they can do, what they get. No features list yet.
2
5–10 MIN
Describe the three core screens
Homepage, main action screen, confirmation/result screen. Three screens are enough for an MVP. Resist adding more.
3
10–20 MIN
Generate and review v0.1
Paste your prompt into Lovable. Review what it built. Note the three most important fixes — not all of them. Three only.
4
20–25 MIN
Fix the three things
Use the chat interface to fix each one in sequence. One instruction per message. Never batch multiple changes.
5
25–30 MIN
Deploy and share the URL
Lovable publishes your app to a live URL automatically. Copy it. Share it. The build is done. Everything else is iteration.

What You'll Learn

Arjun builds a live pre-loved goods marketplace from zero to deployed URL in under 30 minutes — narrating every decision, every prompt, and every iteration in real time. You see the messy middle: what breaks, how he fixes it with a single sentence, and why he stops adding features before the clock runs out.

The second half covers the single biggest mistake first-time builders make — and the mindset shift that separates students who ship from students who plan forever.

What to Avoid
🚫
Don't describe features — describe the user. "A listing page with filters, sort options, and a map view" is a features list. "A page where a buyer can find exactly what they're looking for in under 10 seconds" is a brief. Lovable builds better from the second.
🚫
Don't fix everything at once. Sending 10 change requests in one message breaks the build. One change, one message, check the result, then send the next. This is the discipline of Vibe Coding.
🚫
Don't wait until it's perfect to share. The moment you have a live URL, share it with 5 people and ask "what would stop you from using this?" That feedback is worth more than any additional feature you could add in the same time.
