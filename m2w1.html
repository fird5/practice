What You'll Learn

The average university student reads less than 30% of assigned materials — not from laziness, but because the volume of information is structurally impossible to process linearly. This lesson reframes the problem: you don't need to read more, you need to synthesise smarter.

NotebookLM is Google's AI research assistant built specifically for this task. Upload your sources — PDFs, lecture slides, research papers, YouTube links — and it builds an interactive knowledge base with source-cited answers. Every response links back to the exact page in your original document.

What NotebookLM Can Do
📄
Ingest up to 50 sources per Notebook. PDFs, Google Docs, YouTube videos, web URLs, audio files. Your entire semester reading list in one place.
💬
Answer questions with citations. Every answer links to the exact source page. Essential for academic integrity — you always know where information came from.
🎙️
Generate Audio Overviews. NotebookLM converts your entire Notebook into a podcast-style audio briefing — two AI hosts discussing your material. Study on the move.
📋
Create Study Guides and FAQs. One click produces a structured study guide, exam-style questions, or a full briefing document from all sources combined.

What You'll Learn

Hands-on from the first minute. You will build your first Notebook live — uploading real academic sources and learning the exact prompts that extract maximum value across multiple documents simultaneously.

The core skill: synthesis prompting — asking questions that force the AI to connect ideas across sources, not just summarise one document at a time. This is what turns NotebookLM from a search tool into a genuine thinking partner.

The 5-Step Notebook Setup
1
Create a new Notebook
Go to notebooklm.google.com → New Notebook. Name it by subject — one Notebook per topic keeps your knowledge clean and retrieval fast.
2
Upload all your sources
Drag in PDFs, paste Google Drive links, add YouTube URLs. Aim for at least 5 sources — more sources equals richer cross-source synthesis.
3
Generate the Notebook Guide
Click "Notebook Guide" — NotebookLM auto-produces a structured summary, FAQ, and study guide from all your sources in one pass.
4
Ask synthesis questions
Don't ask "What does Source 1 say?" Ask "What are the three main tensions across all my sources on this topic?" Cross-source questions unlock the real power.
5
Generate your Audio Overview
Hit "Generate Audio Overview" — your entire Notebook becomes a 10–20 minute podcast. Download it. Listen while commuting. This is revision redefined.

What You'll Learn

A live over-the-shoulder demo: Dr. Priya builds a complete research brief from 50 pages of SEA regional policy documents in under 15 minutes — from raw PDFs to a polished 5-minute Audio Overview ready for submission.

You will see exactly how the Notebook is structured, which prompts extract the most value, and how to convert the output into a professional brief. The second half covers the mistakes that kill output quality before your Sprint.

What to Avoid
🚫
Don't upload low-quality scans. NotebookLM reads text, not images. A scanned PDF with no OCR layer returns poor results — always use text-readable PDFs.
🚫
Don't ask single-source questions. "What does this paper say about X?" is a search query. "How do my sources disagree on X?" is a synthesis query. Always push for cross-source answers.
🚫
Don't ignore the citations. Click every citation before using information in an academic submission — verify the AI read the source correctly. NotebookLM is accurate but not infallible.

Synthesise 50 Pages of Regional Policy into a 5-Minute Audio Brief 🎙️

Southeast Asia's policy landscape is one of the most complex in the world — overlapping ASEAN frameworks, national digital economy acts, and rapidly shifting AI governance documents. Your brief: select any real regional policy document of at least 50 pages (ASEAN Digital Masterplan, a national AI strategy, a central bank fintech circular), upload it to NotebookLM alongside at least two supporting sources, and produce a 5-minute Audio Overview brief that a non-specialist executive could listen to and act on. The audio must be accompanied by a 5-point written summary with source citations.

Sprint Tips from Dr. PriyaRead before you begin
▼
🎯
Choose a policy document that directly affects your field of study — a Business student picks an e-commerce regulation, a CS student picks a national AI strategy. Relevance shows domain intelligence and scores higher.
🎯
Your written summary must add analysis, not just repeat the audio. The 5 points should surface the most surprising finding, the biggest policy gap, and one specific implication for your industry. That is Scholar-level work.
🎯
The highest-scoring submissions include a custom instruction before generating the Audio Overview — e.g. "Focus on implications for SMEs in Malaysia, not general findings." Customised output always scores higher than default output.
