What You'll Learn

Most SEA brands invest heavily in visual identity and almost nothing in sonic identity. Yet 70% of brand recall in video content is driven by audio — the music, not the imagery, is what lodges in memory. This is the gap Siti has been exploiting for her clients for twelve years.

Suno AI gives every student the ability to produce custom, royalty-free brand soundtracks in under five minutes. But the output is only as good as the sonic brief. This lesson teaches you to write that brief with the same precision a music director would use.

What You'll Learn

Suno AI reads natural language music descriptions and produces full audio tracks. The difference between a generic output and a usable brand asset is the Sonic Brand Brief — a structured prompt that combines all six sonic variables into a precise, repeatable instruction set.

You will build your first Sonic Brand Brief live — for a fictional SEA FinTech super-app — and iterate it until the output passes Siti's brand deployment test: "Would you be comfortable playing this in a boardroom pitch for a Series A investor?"

Sonic Brand Brief Template — Suno AI
Paste into Suno's style/description field
[BRAND]: Modern Southeast Asian fintech super-app. Serving urban millennials. Positioning: trustworthy, forward-moving, quietly ambitious. [SONIC BRIEF]: BPM: 88 — measured, confident pace. Not rushed. Key: E major — bright but grounded. Instrumentation: Minimal piano melody, subtle gamelan texture in the background, clean electronic bass, light brushed percussion. Mood: The feeling of watching your savings grow. Quiet confidence. A city waking up at 6am. Duration: 30 seconds. Build from sparse to fuller in final 10 seconds. Constraints: No guitars. No drums heavier than brushed snare. No Western folk or acoustic elements. No lyrics. Output: Brand background track suitable for app onboarding screens and investor pitch decks.

What to Avoid
🚫
Don't use mood words without music theory anchors. "Happy" is not a sonic instruction. "Upbeat, 120 BPM, C major, bright acoustic piano" is. Emotion without technical specification produces generic results.
🚫
Don't generate without a constraints clause. Without specifying what the track must NOT include, Suno defaults to the most common interpretation of your genre. The constraints clause is what makes the output distinctly yours.
🚫
Don't submit your first generation. The first output from Suno is version 0.1. Generate 4–5 variations, compare them against your brand brief, and select the one that is most deployable — not most impressive on first listen.

A fictional Southeast Asian "Super-App" wallet — combining payments, savings, and micro-loans for urban millennials across three SEA markets — needs a distinctive sonic identity that communicates trust, modernity, and regional cultural fluency. Sprint Tips from SitiRead before you begin
▼
🎯
Write one Sonic Brand Brief and use it as the base for all three assets — adapting only duration and specific use-case instructions. Consistency across assets is what creates a sonic identity, not three independent experiments.
🎯
Cultural specificity elevates the brief. A FinTech serving Malaysia, Indonesia, and Vietnam simultaneously has a specific cultural context. Reference regional musical elements — kecapi, gamelan undertones, or Vietnamese đàn tranh textures — as a background layer to anchor the identity in SEA.
🎯
Your rationale note is part of the submission — 3–5 sentences explaining why each sonic decision serves the brand. Judges look for creative thinking, not just output quality. Show the strategic reasoning behind the brief.
