What You'll Learn

This lesson sets the foundation of the entire module. You will understand why human creativity is your ultimate competitive edge in an AI-saturated world — and how De Bono's Six Thinking Hats gives you a structured, repeatable system to unlock it.

We explore what "Generic AI" produces when asked to solve a problem — and why it always converges on predictable answers. The goal: find the "Third Way" that AI cannot see.

The Six Hats — Quick Reference
🤍
White Hat
Facts & data only. What do we know?
❤️
Red Hat
Emotions & gut feelings. No justification.
🖤
Black Hat
Caution & judgment. What could go wrong?
💛
Yellow Hat
Optimism & value. Why this will work.
💚
Green Hat
Creativity & new ideas. No limits.
🧡
Blue Hat
Process control. Who wears what, when?

What You'll Learn

Now we go inside the framework. You will learn how to actually run a Six Thinking Hats session — solo or with a group — using Gemini as your thinking partner.

The core skill is Parallel Thinking: all participants wear the same hat simultaneously. This separates emotion from logic, criticism from creativity, and produces far better decisions than typical debate.

Key Principles
🔄
One hat at a time. Everyone wears the same hat simultaneously — no individual ownership of a position.
⚡
Segregate thinking modes. Facts in White Hat only. Emotions in Red Hat only. This prevents cognitive contamination.
🤖
Use Gemini as your Blue Hat. Prompt it to facilitate — manage the sequence, summarise each phase, and push for depth.

What You'll Learn

The applied lesson. You will watch a live over-the-shoulder demo of a Six Hats session run on a real SEA business problem — then run one yourself before the Sprint.

We cover the most common mistakes students make — including letting AI collapse all six hats into one "balanced" answer, which defeats the purpose entirely.

What to Avoid
🚫
Don't let AI wear all six hats at once. "Give me a Six Hats analysis" produces a blended, safe output. Run each hat as a separate prompt.
🚫
Don't skip the Red Hat. Most students feel awkward stating emotions professionally. The Red Hat is where your most valuable insights hide.
🚫
Don't rush the Green Hat. Push back on AI's first ideas. Prompt it three more times. The third response is usually the disruptive one.
