What You'll Learn

Gumloop uses a visual canvas where you connect trigger nodes to action nodes. You define what starts the workflow (a new product, a form submission, a scheduled time) and what happens as a result (an AI writing step, an API call, an email send, a social post). No code. No configuration files. Just nodes and connections.

You will build your first Viral Factory workflow — triggered by a product name and image, producing a landing page, three social ad variants, and a welcome email sequence — all automatically, all in under 20 minutes of setup time.

Trigger-to-Action Thinking
⚡
Every manual task is a workflow waiting to be automated. Before building in Gumloop, list every manual step you do after adding a new product. Each step is a node. Connect them.
🔗
One input, many outputs. The highest-leverage workflows take one piece of information (a product name) and fan out to multiple channels simultaneously. Design for fan-out from the start.
🤖
Insert AI writing nodes between data and output. Raw product data goes into an AI node. The AI node writes the caption, ad copy, or email body. The output node delivers it. AI is the translator between information and communication.

The second half is Build-Off preparation — the strategic mindset for competing in a 2-hour live build sprint where judges reward speed, functionality, and automation depth in equal measure. The students who win the Build-Off are the ones who automate before they iterate.

What to Avoid
🚫
Don't automate before you validate. Build the workflow manually once first. If the manual version works and produces value, then automate it. Automating a broken workflow just produces broken outputs faster.
🚫
Don't connect too many tools in your first workflow. Start with 3 nodes: trigger → AI writing → one output channel. Prove the chain works. Then add more output branches. Complexity compounds errors.
🚫
Don't skip the AI writing prompt node. Raw product data sent directly to an output produces robotic content. Always insert an AI node that rewrites the data in the correct tone for each channel — Instagram copy is not email copy.

Sprint Tips from ArjunRead before you begin
▼
🎯
Map your workflow on paper first. One trigger → three branches → one output per branch. Draw the nodes and connections before opening Gumloop. Students who plan on paper build in half the time.
🎯
Your AI writing nodes must be channel-specific. The Instagram caption node prompt should say "Write an Instagram caption for this coffee product — punchy, emoji-heavy, ends with a CTA." The TikTok node says "Write a 15-second TikTok hook script that creates FOMO." Different channels, different prompts, different outputs.
🎯
Include your workflow diagram in the submission — a screenshot of the Gumloop canvas showing all nodes and connections. Judges evaluate automation architecture, not just output quality. Show the system, not just the results.
